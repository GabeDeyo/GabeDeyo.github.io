Understanding Emotional Intelligence

  • What is Emotional Intelligence

    What do we mean by emotional intelligence?
      - Understanding and expressing our emotions.
      - Being empathetic when communicating with others.

    Emotional intelligence is part of our psychological make-up, along with personalities and IQ.
      - Personality and IQ don't predict emotional intelligence.
      - Anybody can develop emotional intelligence, not always the case with the others.

    Developing emotional intelligence is important because you actually have control over it.
      - This can have a direct impact on your performance and personal achievements, and the relationships you build.
      - Emotional intelligence is sometimes known as EQ (emotional quotient).

    Measuring emotional intelligence focuses on four criteria.
      1. Being aware of emotions.
      2. Expressing emotions.
      3. Controlling emotions.
      4. Handling relationships with others effectively.

    There are four areas of emotional intelligence.
      1. Self-Awareness
      2. Self-Management
      3. Social Awareness
      4. Relationship Development

  • The Role of EQ

    Understanding and managing your emotions is critical if you want to enhance your performance and interact successfully with others.

    There's a chain reaction between our emotions, wich is how we feel about an event, and then our behaviour, which is how we respond to that event.
      - The process starts internal, which means it's going on inside of your head and your body.
      - This then leads to a behavioral reaction, which is how we then interact with others based on how we processed the situation.

    Your level of emotional intelligence will determine how you react to situations, how you face challenges, and how you build relationships.

  • Thinking at the Speed of Light

    It takes conscious practice to become aware of our emotional response process.
      - The first step is being aware of our emotional response process.
      - Next, you need to intercept that response process.
